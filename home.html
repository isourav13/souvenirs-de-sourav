    How is your life

    Today is a Sunday, you've already passed your teenage phase long back. Tomorrow will be office day once again, looking at the footpath there are no signs of the holes where the cricket stumps were used to be mounted. The balcony on the opposite side houses Bablu's small rented room. His mother with gray hair stares back at everyone, longing to reach his dead son, who died of a sudden heart attack 7 years back. Now there is no Bablu to wicket keep behind the stumps. Papan is a genius these days, he is now pursuing his phD. in physics at a certain American university, his colossal contributions to fielding near the rough patch on the road, cannot be forgotten along with science.

    today?

    Whatever fast bowling was done, has to be credited to Bablu. He is already married and doesn't mix with any of his friends except his wife with buck teeth and the pigeons on the roof. Papai is somehow the only friend that comes to your house on weekends and shares his story of how his 65th gf is his true love while sipping on a bottle of Jack Daniels, he was the all-rounder, yet he owns an all variety shop in the bazar. The bijoya sammilani isn't as fun as it used to be, no Pinky was nowhere to be found. She went off to her mamarbari near Shyambazar, after her father died, no showstopper biz no pseudo gf calling whatsoever. The footpath staring can be hypnotic, the first cycle ride, the first game of cricket, the first death, the first absence and first friendship rose from there only. You aren't nostalgic, the footpath is. The kosha mangsho smell is just a necessity to fill your stomach, instead of the reason to bowl the last ball of the match.

    //austere reminiscence

    It is the year 2000, you're watching Titanic being aired on Star movies for the first time on cable tv. The morning newspaper has the face of Jyoti Basu as headline. While you're being prepared for school, your mother packs your tiffin with a bapuji cake, a boiled egg and a banana. You take the as many as you like majority seater cranky public buses with your father, written 38+1 with two double seater seats. You're anxious for what will happen next on Narayan Debnath's action comics on Shuktara magazine throughout the whole class, when you open your tiffin box, you realize that all the contents have been squished unrecognizable, to anonymity. As recess ends you also end up guessing what would be the fate of Goku on Dragon Ball Z and Card captor Sakura. The maths class is extremely boring while you look out of the window ending up smelling rotten bamboos signifying that Durga pujo is near. Your heart beats faster than ever, unlimited Rollick ice-cream and fast food along with volunteering badges for the parar pujo.
